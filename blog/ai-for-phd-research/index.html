Writing a PhD thesis means months of searching through hundreds of PDFs, dozens of Word files with scattered notes, and hours spent verifying citations. The largest part of a PhD student's time doesn't go toward academic thinking — it goes toward logistics.

AI cannot write your thesis. But it can eliminate 60-70% of the logistical work, leaving you free to focus on what matters: your academic argument.

📌 Important: Used correctly, AI in academic research is similar to an advanced search engine + organization assistant. The argument, interpretation and original contribution remain yours.

What AI can do for your PhD thesis

1

Search 7 academic databases simultaneously

ResearchMind AI searches OpenAlex, CrossRef, Semantic Scholar, ArXiv, CORE, Internet Archive and DOAJ in parallel. Unpaywall automatically finds free PDF versions of paywalled articles.

2

Chat with your PDFs — exact page numbers

Import your PDFs and ask questions in natural language. The AI finds the exact passage with page number from your library — processed entirely locally on your computer.

3

Generate a complete academic study

For any topic, the app generates a structured study section by section: historical context, textual analysis, patristic references, complete bibliography.

4

Export to Word with academic formatting

One click exports to .docx with Times New Roman 12pt, 1.5 spacing, justified text, correctly formatted footnotes.

Specific use cases for PhD research

Literature Review

The most time-consuming chapter in any thesis. With ResearchMind AI: enter your topic, get relevant articles from 7 databases, chat with each PDF for details, generate the synthesis — in hours, not weeks.

Theology and patristic research

Internet Archive contains millions of digitized patristic texts — Church Fathers, critical editions, medieval texts — all searchable directly from the app. DOAJ includes peer-reviewed open access theology journals.

Zettelkasten note system

ResearchMind AI includes a Zettelkasten system: notes are linked to their source documents and can be searched semantically. When you return after 6 months, you immediately find the idea and its source.

Knowledge graph

Visualize graphically how documents in your library relate to each other — which sources discuss the same topic, what thematic clusters exist. Useful for structuring thesis chapters.

PhD Progress Dashboard

The app includes a dedicated PhD panel for tracking thesis progress: chapters written, bibliography accumulated, objectives. An overview of your entire thesis at any time.
